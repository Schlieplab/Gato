Depth-First-Search (DFS) Traversal

This algorithm traverses a graph in depth-first order and visualizes the predecessor tree being built and the DFS vertex labels. See Chapter 2 in the CATBox book. Recursive implementation.

Visualization

Vertex Colors
    
Initial color for vertices that have neither been visited nor processed .
    
Vertices of this color have been processed ; i.e., their neighbors have been explored.
    
The vertices being processed are displayed with a wide outline in this color. Note that multiple vertices are processed simultaneously.
Edge Colors
    
Initial color for edges. Edges of this color have not been traversed yet.
    
Color of the edge being traversed .
    
Traversed edges displayed in the color are part of the BFS tree.
    
Color of edges traversed which are not part of the BFS tree.
Further elements
    
The DFS label assigned to visited vertices is displayed in this color.
